The Infrastructure

Setting up the foundation for effective cold outbound campaigns

If the world's best cold email is sent but it never reaches the recipient's primary inbox, the email is worthless. Infrastructure is a prerequisite for good outbound - you might succeed with mediocre emails and great infrastructure, but you'll never succeed with great emails if your infrastructure doesn't work.

  1. Buy and Set Up Adjacent Domains

    Purchase domains similar to your company's main domain to ensure deliverability at scale

    • Buy domains that are similar to your company's main domain (e.g., getcompany.com, withcompany.com)
    • Redirect these domains to your main website
    • Calculate how many domains you need based on your desired email volume
    • Use the formula: Adjacent Domains = (Desired Daily Volume / 60) x 1.1

  2. Optimize Your Domains for Deliverability

    Set up authentication records to boost your domain authority with email service providers

    • Set up SPF records to specify which mail servers can send email on behalf of your domain
    • Configure DKIM to add a digital signature that verifies your emails haven't been tampered with
    • Implement DMARC to tell receiving servers how to handle authentication failures
    • These steps boost your authenticity in the eyes of email service providers like Gmail

  3. Create Email Inboxes on Your Domains

    Set up the right infrastructure for sending emails from your domains

    • Set up 3 inboxes per domain to reduce risk and diversify sending
    • Use Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 (avoid private IP solutions)
    • Create separate Google Workspace accounts for different domains (2 domains per account max)
    • Use VPNs and antidetect browsers when setting up multiple accounts
    • Use real human names for your email accounts, preferably company leaders

  4. Warm Up Your Email Inboxes

    Establish a positive reputation for your new email accounts before sending outbound

    • Send emails from your accounts to other email accounts that engage positively
    • Create a profile of activity matching what a normal good email inbox looks like
    • Warm from the same IP you'll be sending outbound from
    • Start with lower volume and gradually increase over several weeks
    • Continue warming activities even after you start sending cold outbound

Why Skip This At Your Peril

Many outbound campaigns fail at the infrastructure stage. Most companies use a single inbox per person approach, which is inefficient and risky:

  • ESPs want to see human-like volume from email accounts (not hundreds of identical emails)
  • Sending from your main domain puts your entire company's email reputation at risk
  • Without proper authentication records, your emails are more likely to be flagged as spam
  • When you're sending at scale, diversifying across domains protects your deliverability

Writing Great Emails

Crafting cold emails that get positive, revenue-generating replies

Deciding Who To Email

Focus on finding people who are actively feeling the pain your product solves:

  • Ask: "Who is currently feeling the pain that our product solves?"
  • Look for signals that indicate pain (hiring patterns, tools used, etc.)
  • For small companies (<50 people), email the founder directly
  • For larger companies, target decision-makers and higher-ups
  • Email multiple people at the same company when appropriate

Getting The Ideas Right

Focus your email on addressing one of these two key questions:

  • How will you make their company more money?
  • How will you make this specific person's life easier/better?
  • Use debate-style contentions to strengthen your arguments
  • Structure with Claim, Warrants, Evidence, and Impact
  • Make sure each argument is solid before including it in your email

Elements of Great Cold Emails

Most effective cold emails include these components:

  • Personalization: Something that makes the email feel 1:1 and not mass-sent
  • Strong claim: A compelling statement about the value you provide
  • Evidence: Proof points that back up your claim
  • Clear next step: A specific action for the recipient to take
  • Brevity: Keep it under 200 words to respect their time

Example Cold Email That Worked

Hey [Name] — Congrats on the recent raise (Lachy Groom also funded a previous company of mine, was generally helpful). Happy to see that [Company] looks a bit different from all the generic/gimmicky AI support startups out there. Looked at your job board and noticed you're hiring for sales. Imagine you're planning to use outbound to gain significant early/high ROI traction? If so - you should check out Za-zu, my company. It's an email sequencer that lets one smart growth person send personalized outbound at the scale of 50 SDRs. I'd love to show you the product. Would it be crazy to chat this week? Matt

Nailing the Style, Tone, and Jargon

The style of your email matters as much as the content:

  • Adjust your tone based on your audience (more casual for startups, more formal for enterprise)
  • Don't try to fake a casual tone with unprofessional language
  • Test a slightly more casual tone than you think is needed
  • Learn and use industry-specific jargon correctly
  • Refer to companies the way insiders do
  • Write subject lines that feel like internal emails
  • Use a profile picture to increase open rates

Crafting High-Converting Subject Lines

Your subject line is the gatekeeper to your email - it determines whether your message gets opened or ignored:

Subject Line Best Practices

  • Keep it short (4-7 words, 40-60 characters)
  • Create curiosity without being clickbait
  • Include personalization when possible (name, company)
  • Align with the email content (don't mislead)
  • Use lowercase for a more personal feel
  • Avoid spam trigger words and excessive punctuation

Subject Line Formulas That Work

  • Referral: "Intro from [mutual connection]"
  • Personalized observation: "Your [specific detail] caught my attention"
  • Value proposition: "Quick idea for [specific pain point]"
  • Question: "Question about [relevant topic]"
  • Praise: "Impressed by [something they did]"
  • Timely: "Following up on [recent event/news]"

Example Subject Lines That Worked

• "[Name], quick question about [Company]'s sales process"
• "your AI strategy"
• "saw you're hiring SDRs"
• "congrats on the series B"
• "[Mutual connection] suggested we connect"
• "impressed by your recent launch"
• "3 thoughts on your [specific challenge]"

A/B Testing Subject Lines

Testing different subject lines is critical for optimizing open rates:

  • Test 3-5 variations for each email campaign
  • Change only one variable at a time (length, personalization, question vs. statement)
  • Send each variant to a statistically significant sample
  • Measure both open rates and response rates (sometimes higher open rates lead to lower response rates)
  • Document learnings about what works for your specific audience

Sending Personalized Outbound at Scale

Using AI to automate highly effective, personalized cold emails

AI-Powered Personalization

Use AI to fill in the personalized elements of your emails at scale:

  1. Write a dream 1:1, perfectly personalized email
  2. Identify the elements that need personalization
  3. Train AI to replace those elements with personalized content
  4. Test extensively to ensure quality before launching

AI can handle different levels of personalization:

  • Simple copy and paste (first name, company name)
  • Summarization and normalization of data
  • Conditional content based on if-then logic
  • More complex personalized elements like industry-specific insights

Campaign Structure

Effective campaigns have a thoughtful multi-stage structure:

  • Create 4-6 stages (individual emails in sequence)
  • Write 3-6 variants for each stage to test different approaches
  • Space emails appropriately (2 days, 4 days, 7 days, etc.)
  • Create a narrative arc across the entire campaign
  • Use different messaging angles in different variants
  • Implement spintaxing to avoid identical emails

Prompting AI Effectively

Get high-quality personalization from AI with these techniques:

Instructions

  • Start with clear input data for AI to work with
  • Write concise, specific instructions
  • Include helpful Dos and Don'ts to guide output
  • Test repeatedly and refine your prompts

Examples

  • Provide input-output pairs as examples
  • Include multiple positive examples
  • Show both the input data and the desired output
  • Create a persona for the AI to adopt

Optimizing Based on Data

Use campaign performance data to iteratively improve your results:

  • Track open rates and reply rates across stages and variants
  • Classify replies as positive or negative
  • When a variant significantly outperforms others, redirect volume to it
  • Create new variants based on what's working best
  • Test one variable at a time to identify what's driving success
  • Be willing to completely rethink your messaging if data suggests it

Infrastructure & Setup for Scalable Phone Outreach

Setting up the foundation for an effective cold calling operation

Tools & Technology

The right tech stack makes cold calling more efficient and effective:

  • Use a CRM as your system of record (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.)
  • Implement a cloud-based VoIP phone system with CRM integration
  • Consider power dialers to increase call volume (with TCPA compliance)
  • Set up call recording for training (with proper compliance)
  • Use local presence dialing to improve answer rates by up to 30%

Call Scheduling & Prioritization

Optimize when and who you call for maximum effectiveness:

  • Call during peak hours - early morning (8-9am) or late afternoon (4-5pm)
  • Focus on mid-week days (Tuesday-Thursday) for better connect rates
  • Prioritize high-value prospects with buying signals or recent engagement
  • Schedule dedicated "power hour" blocks during optimal calling times
  • Segment prospects by time zone for efficient scheduling

Building the Outbound Team

Structure your team for cold calling success:

  • Hire SDRs who are persistent, personable, and organized
  • Create realistic KPIs (e.g., 50 calls per day, 10-15 appointments per month)
  • Implement call coaching and peer learning systems
  • Consider a "pod structure" where SDRs, AEs, and others work together on accounts
  • Use team dashboards to track calls, connect rates, and meeting sets

Creating Personalized, Value-Driven Conversations

Turn cold calls into meaningful conversations that drive results

Consultative Framework

Build your call around a consultative approach:

  1. Rapport & Relevance: Quick personal connection to start
  2. Problem Discovery: Open questions to uncover challenges
  3. Value Proposition: Tailored to their specific needs
  4. Engagement/Qualification: Targeted questions to gauge fit
  5. Next Step (Call to Action): Clear proposal for continuing

Question Techniques

Master the art of effective questioning:

  • Situation: "How are you currently managing X?"
  • Problem: "What challenges are you facing with that setup?"
  • Implication: "How does that affect your team's productivity?"
  • Need-Payoff: "If you could solve that, what would it mean?"
  • Listen more than you talk - aim for 40/60 talk ratio (you/them)

Example Opening Lines That Work

Effective ways to start your calls:

The Permission Opener: "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I know you weren't expecting my call – did I catch you at a bad time?"

The Personalized Insight Opener: "Hi [Name], [Your Name] calling from [Company]. I saw that [Prospect Company] recently [announced a new product / expanded to a new market]. Congrats! The reason I'm calling is we've helped others in your industry during expansions like that..."

The Trigger Event Opener: "Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name] with [Company]. I've been speaking with a few [Industry] companies lately – many are struggling with [specific issue]. Is that something you're grappling with too?"

Handling Objections & Increasing Conversions

Turn pushback into opportunities and convert calls into meetings

Common Objections & Responses

How to handle the most frequent objections:

  • "I'm not interested" - Acknowledge and probe: "Is it because you already have a solution, or is now just not a good time?"
  • "Send me some information" - "Absolutely. To make sure it's relevant, is there something particular you'd like information on?"
  • "We already have a solution" - "Many companies we work with started with [Competitor]. What they found was..." (differentiate without disparaging)
  • "No budget" - Pivot to ROI: "I understand. Would it help to see how similar companies achieved a 3x return on investment?"
  • "Not the right time" - Get specific: "I understand timing is important. When would be a better time to discuss this?"

Conversion Techniques

Psychological principles to increase call-to-meeting conversion:

  • Reciprocity: Offer value first (advice, insight) to increase receptivity
  • Social Proof: Share stories of similar companies that benefited
  • De-risking: Make next steps feel safe and low-commitment
  • Foot-in-the-Door: Start with a small ask before larger requests
  • Assumptive Close: "When we meet next week, I'll show you..."

The Perfect Meeting Ask

How to close effectively for the next step:

"Based on what you've shared about [their pain point], I think it would be valuable to show you how [Your Company] has helped other [industry] companies solve this exact issue. How about we schedule a 30-minute demo next [day] at [time]? I'll bring in our solution specialist who can answer any technical questions you might have."

This approach is specific, value-focused, and provides a clear time suggestion - making it easy for the prospect to say yes.

Follow-Up Strategies & Lead Nurturing

Turn initial outreach into ongoing relationships

Voicemail Strategy

Leave voicemails that get callbacks:

  • Keep messages brief (20-30 seconds maximum)
  • Mention a specific pain point or goal
  • Include your contact information clearly
  • Promise a follow-up email (and send it)
  • Use voicemails to build name recognition, even if they don't call back

Multi-Channel Approach

Combine calls with other channels for better results:

  • Follow the "Call -> Voicemail -> Email" same-day sequence
  • Add LinkedIn connection requests with personalized notes
  • Space contacts appropriately (2-3 days between touches)
  • Vary your message slightly with each contact
  • Use email tracking to identify engagement signals for follow-up calls

Persistence That Pays Off

Follow up strategically to maximize results:

  • It takes an average of 8 call attempts to reach a prospect
  • Aim for 6-8 touches over 2-3 weeks for high-potential prospects
  • Segment follow-up by call outcome (no answer, spoke but no meeting, etc.)
  • Always anchor follow-ups to something from previous contacts
  • For long-term nurturing, set calendar reminders for periodic personal follow-ups

Pro Tip: When a prospect says "Call me back in [timeframe]," make a note and follow up exactly when they suggested. Starting with "I'm calling back as you requested" dramatically increases engagement.

Key Metrics & Compliance

Track performance and ensure legal compliance

Critical KPIs to Monitor

Track these metrics to optimize your calling program:

  • Dial/Call Volume: Number of calls made per day/rep
  • Connect Rate: Percentage of calls that reach a human
  • Call-to-Meeting Rate: Percentage of conversations that convert to meetings
  • Average Call Duration: Longer calls often indicate better engagement
  • Pipeline Generated: Dollar value of opportunities from calls

Legal Compliance Essentials

Stay on the right side of the law:

  • TCPA (US): Be cautious with auto-dialers to cell phones
  • Call Hours: Only call between 8am-9pm local time
  • Do-Not-Call: Maintain internal DNC list and honor opt-outs
  • GDPR (EU): Have a lawful basis (legitimate interest) for B2B calls
  • Call Recording: Understand two-party consent laws if recording

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I start with email or phone for cold outreach?

Both channels have their strengths, and the most effective approach is often using them in combination. Email works well for initial contact at scale, while phone calls excel at creating real-time conversations. Consider your specific situation: For reaching executives, phones might work better (57% of C-level execs prefer calls). For technical audiences who prefer written information, email might be better to start. The ideal approach is a coordinated multi-channel strategy where emails and calls reinforce each other - for example, sending an email followed by a call reference the email, or calling first and then sending a follow-up email with promised information.

How many emails should I send per day?

It depends on your infrastructure setup. With the approach described in this handbook, we recommend sending about 20 emails per day per inbox. With 3 inboxes per domain, that's 60 emails per domain per day. Scale your operation by adding more domains rather than increasing the send rate per inbox. This approach mimics natural human sending patterns and reduces the risk of being flagged as spam.

How many calls should I make per day?

For B2B SaaS outreach, a typical SDR should aim for 40-60 calls per day, resulting in roughly 8-12 meaningful conversations. This volume allows for quality conversations while maintaining sufficient activity levels. However, the number can vary based on your target audience, the complexity of your offering, and how much research is needed before each call. Quality always trumps quantity - 30 well-researched, highly targeted calls will yield better results than 100 generic ones. Many successful teams organize calling into "power hour" blocks of focused activity rather than spreading calls throughout the day.

How long should I warm my email accounts before sending cold outbound?

You should warm your email accounts for at least 3-4 weeks before starting cold outbound campaigns. The warming process should start slowly and gradually increase to your target volume. Continue warming activities even after you begin sending cold outbound to maintain a healthy sender reputation. The key is to create a pattern of engagement that matches what a normal, well-received email account would look like.

What's the ideal cold email length?

The ideal cold email should be under 200 words. Shorter emails tend to perform better as they respect the recipient's time and are more likely to be read completely. Focus on making every word count - include personalization, a strong value proposition, brief evidence, and a clear call to action. Avoid lengthy introductions or detailed explanations that can be saved for follow-up conversations.

What's the best time of day to make cold calls?

Research consistently shows that early morning (8:00-9:30 AM) and late afternoon (4:00-5:30 PM) yield the highest connect rates for B2B cold calling. During these times, prospects are often at their desks but not in meetings. Mid-day hours typically see lower answer rates. Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday tend to be the most productive days for cold calling, while Monday mornings (when people are catching up on emails) and Friday afternoons (when people are wrapping up for the weekend) generally have lower success rates. Always test and track results for your specific audience, as optimal times can vary by industry.

How do I avoid being flagged as spam?

To avoid being flagged as spam: 1) Set up proper authentication records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), 2) Warm up your email accounts properly, 3) Maintain human-like sending patterns and volume, 4) Personalize your emails genuinely, 5) Use multiple domains to diversify risk, 6) Avoid spam trigger words and excessive punctuation, 7) Test your emails before large sends, and 8) Monitor your deliverability and engagement metrics consistently. If you see signs of deliverability issues, pause sending and address them immediately.

How many follow-up attempts should I make?

For B2B SaaS outreach, persistence pays off. Data shows it takes an average of 8 attempts to reach a prospect and 5-7 touches to convert them to a meeting. A good outreach sequence typically includes 6-8 total touchpoints (combination of emails, calls, and social touches) over 2-3 weeks. For high-value prospects, you might extend this further. The key is to add value with each touch rather than simply saying "just checking in." Space your follow-ups appropriately - perhaps 2-3 days between early touches, then extending to 5-7 days for later attempts. Remember to vary your approach and channels throughout the sequence.
